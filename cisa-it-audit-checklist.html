Introduction

From an IT expert's perspective, an IT audit for a SACCO serves as a critical assurance function to evaluate and strengthen the organization's technology posture.

The primary goals of this audit are to:

  • Ensure the confidentiality, integrity, and availability (CIA) of member and financial data.
  • Verify compliance with regulatory requirements such as CBK guidelines, Data Protection Act (Kenya), and ISACA standards.
  • Assess risk management effectiveness across IT infrastructure and operations.
  • Evaluate the maturity of SACCO's IT governance, security controls, and disaster preparedness.
  • Support strategic decision-making through identification of technology weaknesses and optimization opportunities.

This checklist is designed to guide auditors, IT managers, and SACCO stakeholders through a comprehensive and structured evaluation process.

IT Audit Requirements

Before commencing the IT audit, ensure the following documentation and access rights are available to facilitate a smooth and thorough review:

  • Organizational Structure: IT department organogram and reporting lines.
  • IT Policies and Procedures: Security, acceptable use, backup, incident response, and change management.
  • Network Diagram and Asset Inventory: Updated topology, IP schemes, hardware/software inventory.
  • Access to Logs: System logs, firewall logs, antivirus, and SIEM data.
  • Audit Trail of Core Banking System (CBS): Including changes, access attempts, and financial transactions.
  • Change Management Records: Approved requests, testing outcomes, and rollback plans.
  • Contracts and SLAs: For outsourced services (e.g., data center hosting, support vendors).
  • Evidence of Staff Training: Cybersecurity, data protection, system usage.
  • Prior Audit Reports: Internal and external audit findings and status of recommendations.

1. Governance and Management of IT (CISA Domain: Governance & IT Management)

  • Documented IT Strategic Plan aligned with SACCO objectives
    Confirm IT strategy supports business goals like financial inclusion, member satisfaction, and mobile banking expansion.
  • IT policies and procedures documented, reviewed, and approved
    Include acceptable use, password policy, BYOD, email policy, backup policy, and incident handling.
  • IT organizational structure, roles, and responsibilities clearly defined
    Ensure clear separation between operations, development, and security roles (e.g., no system admin should be a teller).
  • Formal IT steering committee or board-level oversight
    Review meeting minutes and decisions showing IT performance, risk management, and project alignment.
  • Regulatory and legal compliance monitored (e.g., SASRA, Data Protection Act)
    Evidence of regular compliance audits and updates to accommodate changes in regulation.

2.IT Infrastructure, Operations & Maintenance)

  • Up-to-date hardware/software inventory with asset tracking
    Include laptops, servers, routers, firewalls, mobile devices, and core banking software licenses.
  • Network architecture and security configurations documented
    Include secure configurations of routers, firewalls, VPNs for branch interconnectivity.
  • Patch and vulnerability management processes in place
    Confirm system updates are documented, automated where possible, and tested before deployment.
  • Performance monitoring and capacity planning tools in use
    Assess whether systems can scale during peak times (e.g., dividend payouts, year-end closures).
  • Job scheduling and batch processing controls implemented
    Especially relevant for . using legacy batch systems for overnight updates.

3. Protection of Information Assets

  • Information security governance framework established
    Confirm existence of an ISMS (Information Security Management System) based on ISO/IEC 27001 or similar.
  • Access controls (logical and physical) enforced
    Role-based access in the core banking system, secure authentication (MFA), and periodic access reviews.
  • Security solutions implemented (Firewall, IDS/IPS, Antivirus, DLP)
    Must be active, licensed, and configured to alert or block anomalies.
  • Data encryption (at rest and in transit) and masking techniques used
    Especially important for internet/mobile banking platforms and offsite backups.
  • Incident response plan documented and tested
    Evidence of recent drills or real incidents managed successfully.
  • User awareness and security training regularly conducted
    Including phishing simulation, social engineering awareness, and password hygiene.

4. Systems Development and Maintenance

  • Formal SDLC methodology adopted and enforced
    Review documented phases: requirements, design, development, testing, deployment, and maintenance.
  • Change management and version control implemented
    Evidence of change requests, approvals, testing, and rollback procedures.
  • Segregation of duties in development, testing, and production environments
    Developers should not deploy code directly to production.
  • Penetration testing and code reviews regularly conducted
    Security vulnerabilities identified and remediated timely.
  • System backups and rollback plans for new releases
    Ensure no disruption to SACCO operations during deployments.

5. Backup and Disaster Recovery)

  • Backup policies defined, documented, and tested
    Regular full and incremental backups of core banking data, financial records, and member info.
  • Offsite and secure storage of backups
    Including encrypted cloud storage or physical secure locations.
  • Disaster recovery (DR) plan developed and tested
    Evidence of recent DR drills and updated recovery point/time objectives (RPO/RTO).
  • Business continuity planning aligned with IT DR
    Clear roles, communication plans, and alternative work arrangements for SACCO staff.

6. Audit and Monitoring

  • Regular IT audits conducted with documented findings
    Internal audit, external auditors (e.g., KPMG, PWC), and follow-up actions tracked.
  • Ensure logs are retained for regulatory requirements
    Often 5 years for financial records with protected log integrity.
  • Internal and external audit reports reviewed and actioned
    Audit findings, risk registers, and remediation timelines tracked.
  • Continuous monitoring tools and vulnerability scans used
    Automated security monitoring for network, application, and endpoint security.

7. Physical and Environmental Security

  • Server rooms and data centers physically secured
    Access control, CCTV surveillance, and environmental controls (fire suppression, HVAC, humidity).
  • Workstations secured and maintained
    Screen locks, antivirus updates, and clean desk policies in SACCO branches.
  • Power backup and surge protection in place
    UPS systems, generators, and regular maintenance logs.
  • Secure disposal of hardware and sensitive media
    Documented destruction/shredding of old drives, tapes, and printed financial reports.

8. Compliance, Audit Trails & Documentation

  • Policies, procedures, and standards documented and accessible
    Include IT policies, code of conduct, and audit guidelines.
  • Training records maintained for all IT personnel
    Security awareness, technical certifications, and compliance workshops.
  • Third-party vendor management policies
    Contracts, SLAs, and security assessments of service providers.
  • Regular review and update of IT controls
    Ensure controls remain effective with emerging risks and technology changes.
